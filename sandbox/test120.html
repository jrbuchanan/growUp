Genesis 1.1-2.25


Genesis 1 and 2 are key chapters for understanding God and His relationship to mankind. These two chapters reveal that God is the sovereign, all-powerful creator of the universe and that He created man in His image. Part of what this means is that we are created to have a relationship with God.

Genesis 3.1-24


Up until Genesis 3, the world God created was perfect and untarnished by decay. But in this chapter we will see that something has gone horribly wrong. Our first parents, Adam and Eve rebelled against God and brought death and destruction into the world.

Genesis 6:5-7:24


These passages reveal the continuing effects and sin, showing how the corruption that began in Adam and Eve has spread to all mankind. Within just a few generations, the world had become so corrupt that God was sorry that He had ever created the world. So he decides to destroy his creation, but one man and his family found grace in the eyes of the Lord.

Genesis 8:1-9:17


In this passage we will see how God delivered Noah and his family through the flood and established the first of several OT covenants. In this covenant, God promises that He will never destroy the entire earth again by flood.

Genesis 11:1-9


This passage continues to show man's rebellion against God. It is unclear exactly what these ancient people had in mind when building the tower of Babel, but it is clear that God took it as a sign of arrogance and rebellion. Here we see the orgin of the various languages and ethnic groups that exist across the world. It is interesting to note that what God confuses here in Genesis 11, he eventually reconciles in Revelation 5.

Genesis 22:1-22:19


In this passage Abraham's faith is tested when he is called upon to offer his son as a sacrifice to God. Read Hebrews 11:17-19 to see why Abraham was willing to obey God. This is one of the greatest pictures of Christ in the Old Testament. The lamb that takes Isaac's place is a symbol of the way the Jesus would later become our subsitute on the cross.

Genesis 27:1-28:22


These passages show how the covenant with Abraham was passed down to the next generations. Just as He had been faithful to Abraham over the course of his lifetime, the Lord is also faithful to Abraham's descendants. God's faithfulness to His promises is contrasted with the deceitful character of Jacob.
